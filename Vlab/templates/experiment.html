Introduction

  • Specific heat is the amount of heat added to raise the temperature of unit mass by 1 degree Celsius.
  • The specific heat of any substance is same in solid and liquid phase.
  • However, depending on the process of heat transfer, the gas has two specific heats: the specific heat at constant volume (Cv) and the specific heat at constant pressure ({C}_{p}).
  • The normal values of specific heats of air at atmospheric conditions are, Cp = 1.005 kJ/kgK and Cv = 0.718 kJ/kgK.
  • The specific heats are functions of temperature. However, with the extremely high temperature range experienced in internal combustion and gas turbine engines one can obtain significant errors.
  • The heat gained by the material is, Q = m Cp Δ T
  • Where, Q = amount of heat added, m = mass of the object and Δ T = temperature difference.


Procedure
Construction/Arrangment
The apparatus consists of a blower unit fitted with a test pipe. The test section is surrounded by an electrical heater. Two thermocouples are mounted in the air stream one at the entrance and the other at the exit of the test section to measure the air temperature. One thermocouple is mounted on the test rig to measure the atmospheric air temperature. Test pipe is connected to the delivery side of the blower along with an orifice to measure the flow of air through the pipe. Airflow is measured with the help of this orifice meter and the water manometer fitted on the board. Input to the heater is given through a dimmerstat. It is to be noted that, only a part of the total heat supplied is utilized in heating the air.

Procedure
  1. Switch ON the mains.
  2. Switch ON the blower.
  3. Adjust the flow by means of gate valve to some desired difference in the manometer level.
  4. Switch ON the heater.
  5. Start the heating of the test With the help of dimmerstat, adjust desired heat input. Measure voltage and current with the help of voltmeter and ammeter respectively.
  6. Take readings of all the three thermocouples at the intervals of 10 minutes until the steady state condition is reached.
  7. Note down the heat input.

Precautions
  1. Keep the dimmerstat at zero position before switching ON the power supply.
  2. Increase the voltmeter gradually.
  3. Do not stop the blower in between the testing periods.
  4. Do not disturb thermocouples while Operate selector switch of the temperature indicator gently. Don’t exceed 200 watts.

Calculations

Volume flow rate, v m3/s,
v = Cd × A0 ×A1 × [√2 × g × h × (ρw / ρa)] /(√A 2 – A 2)
Where,
A0 = Area of cross section of orifice in m2
A0 = (π/4) × d2
Cd = Coefficient of discharge of orifice = 0.68
A1 = Area of cross section of the pipe at entry section in m2
ρw = Density of water = 1,000 kg/m3
ρa = Density of air at ambient temperature = 1.2 kg/m3
h = Water manometer reading in meter
Mass flow rate of air, ma kg/s,
ma = v × ρa
ρa = Density of air at ambient temperature = 1.2 kg/m3
Temperature rise in air in 0C or K, ΔT
ΔT = (To – Ti)
Heat Supplied, Q
Q = V × I J/s
V = Voltage in volts, I = Current in ampere
Heat carried away by air, Qa J/s,
Qa = ma × Cp × ΔT
Heat supplied is equal to the heat absorbed by the air,
Q = Qa
V × I = ma × Cp × ΔT
Thus, Cp = (V × I) / (ma × ΔT)
